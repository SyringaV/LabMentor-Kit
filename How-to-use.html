How to Use the cell counting feature?

  1. Upload Hemocytometer Image: Click the "Upload Image" button to select and upload an image of the hemocytometer. Use the zoom and pan controls to adjust the image for better visibility.
  2. Count Cells: Click on the numbers corresponding to the squares where you've counted cells. You can choose to count cells in either 4 or 5 squares for accuracy.
  3. Fill Up the Table: Once you've selected the squares, a table will appear. Fill in the numbers of viable and non-viable cells you observe under the microscope for each selected square.
  4. Provide Sample Information: Enter the volume of the original cell sample and the volume of the diluting liquid used. This information is necessary for calculating the dilution factor.
  5. View Calculations: After entering the sample information, the calculation will automatically show the results, including the total cell count, concentration, and any other relevant metrics.
  6. Review and Interpret Results: Take a moment to review the calculated results. These values can provide valuable insights into cell density and viability, which are crucial for various research and diagnostic applications.
  7. Repeat as Needed: You can repeat the process as necessary for different cell samples or experiments. Ensure accuracy by carefully following the counting and data entry steps outlined above.
  8. Troubleshooting: If you encounter any issues or discrepancies during the counting process, refer to troubleshooting tips or reach out to support for assistance.
